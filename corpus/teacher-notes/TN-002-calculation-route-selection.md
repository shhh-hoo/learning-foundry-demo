# Selecting a calculation route

Most 9701 stoichiometric calculations can be represented as a path through quantities:

```text
observable quantity
→ amount in moles
→ stoichiometric relationship
→ amount in moles
→ requested quantity
```

The first and last conversions depend on the evidence in the question.

| Evidence | Convert to amount using |
|---|---|
| mass | `n = m / M` |
| solution volume and concentration | `n = cV` with `V` in dm³ |
| gas volume at stated molar volume | `n = V / Vm` |
| gas pressure, volume and temperature | `n = pV / RT` |
| particles | `n = N / L` |

## Route-selection sequence

1. Identify the target quantity and unit.
2. Identify all candidate starting quantities.
3. Convert relevant candidates to moles.
4. If more than one reactant is supplied, determine the limiting reagent.
5. Apply the balanced-equation ratio.
6. Convert the target amount into the requested quantity.
7. Apply purity or yield only at the correct stage.
8. Check units and significant figures.

The route is not chosen from keywords alone. It is chosen from the available evidence and the target.
