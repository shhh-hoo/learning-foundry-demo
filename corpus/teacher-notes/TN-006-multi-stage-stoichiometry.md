# Multi-stage stoichiometry

A multi-stage problem should be represented as a quantity graph rather than one memorised formula.

Example structure:

```text
sample mass
→ pure-component amount
→ reaction product amount
→ aliquot amount
→ titration amount
→ requested purity or concentration
```

## Strategy

1. Write the target at the right side of the page.
2. Mark every measured quantity and its unit.
3. Identify each physical transformation: dilution, aliquot, reaction, gas collection or titration.
4. Draw one directed edge for each justified conversion.
5. Do not cross an edge until the evidence needed for that conversion is present.
6. Evaluate limiting reagent before propagating product amount.
7. Apply dilution, purity and yield factors exactly once.

## Diagnostic principle

The first pedagogical issue is the earliest invalid edge in the learner's quantity graph, not necessarily the first arithmetic error visible in the written lines.
