# Percentage purity

Percentage purity compares the amount of the target substance with the total sample.

```text
percentage purity =
mass of pure target substance / mass of sample × 100
```

The measured evidence may not directly give the pure mass. A typical route is:

```text
measured gas or titration result
→ moles of measured species
→ stoichiometric ratio
→ moles of pure substance
→ mass of pure substance
→ percentage purity
```

## Keep purity separate from yield

- purity asks what fraction of the original sample is the target substance;
- yield asks what fraction of the theoretical product was actually obtained.

## Common errors

- dividing by product mass instead of sample mass;
- applying the percentage before recovering the amount of pure substance;
- using a gas volume without converting it under the stated conditions;
- assuming every component of the sample reacts in the same way.
