# Limiting reagent

The limiting reagent is the reactant that can complete the fewest reaction batches.

For each reactant:

```text
reaction batches = available amount / stoichiometric coefficient
```

The smallest value identifies the limiting reagent.

## Why mass or volume alone is not enough

A smaller mass can contain more moles if its molar mass is smaller. A smaller volume can also contain more moles if its concentration is larger. Compare amount relative to the equation coefficient, not the raw measurements.

## Common errors

- selecting the smaller mass;
- converting only one reactant to moles;
- comparing moles without dividing by coefficients;
- calculating product from an excess reagent;
- forgetting to subtract the amount consumed when asked for excess remaining.

## Diagnostic evidence

A valid limiting-reagent diagnosis needs:

- the balanced equation;
- the initial amount or convertible evidence for every relevant reactant;
- the target or decision requested.
